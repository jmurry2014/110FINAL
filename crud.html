What is CRUD?

In the computer programming world, CRUD is an acronym worth knowing. CRUD is an acronym that stands for Create, Read, Update and Delete.Think of CRUD as a simple concept that represents the four basic functions that models should be able to do and are considered necessary to implement a persistent storage application. In simpler terms, it represents the four basic operations you can do on any data. You can create something new, read or view the newly created data, edit or update the data and finally the option to delete it. I will go over each fuction with an example walk through of a simple ordering system, more specifically door dash.

Create

Once you find the food your looking for on an app you will want to click on your cart to proceed to checkout. Once you finalize your order you will have then CREATED your order for the doordash database

Read

After creating your order you want to verify that your order has been placed so you head over to the recent orders screen where you can READ all the details about your order.

Update

After reviewing your order you get sidetracked by suggested items at the bottom of the screen. You realize you dont have a drink for your order. You quickly add this item to your cart before time is to late. You have now succesfully Updated your cart.

Delete

You go to your fridge for a drink of water when you realized you had a whole 2 liter in the fridge so you decide to DELETE the drink from your order.

Conclusion

However simple these four actions may seem, they can be found everywhere because they add important functionality that are vital to modern day web development. Without these functionalities your website can and will feel incomplete, and it will drive bad reviews.
